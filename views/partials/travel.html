Lafayette Hotel

Relaxed and ready for anything, the newly redesigned Lafayette Hotel, Swim Club & Bungalows blends unique style and exceptional service with eco-friendly practices to become your ideal travel companion. Reserve a room and see for yourself why this California boutique hotel was a one-time haven for iconic celebrities and continues to be a destination worth discovering again and again. The discount rate is $149/night. Parking is free. Cab ride to the airport is about $20. Reservation must be booked 1 month in advance (May 14th) to qualify for the special rate. The Lafayette Hotel is a 5 minute drive or 15 minute walk from Queen Bee's. For questions, feel free to email coleen@lafayettehotelsd.com.

Porto Vista Hotel

A San Diego California Hotel perfectly located in one of San Diego’s oldest districts, Little Italy, with fine dining restaurants and local pubs and bars along India St and our very own award winning restaurant the Glass Door Restaurant and Bar on the Fourth Floor with amazing harbor views and spectacular Sunsets from our dining patio. The Porto Vista’s great location is close by downtown, Gaslamp District, the San Diego Airport, San Diego Zoo, Sea World and Convention Center. The Porto Vista Hotel is a 5 minute drive to Harbor Island Park and the airport. The group rate is $139/night and includes several perks, but hotel parking is extra ($12/day). The hotel provides and airport shuttle. Reserve by May 12th to qualify for the special group rate.

AirBnB

Victor and I have had good experiences with this site. You can reserve a private room in a home for as low as $27/night or rent out an entire villa for the whole week. Airbnb is a community marketplace where guests can book spaces from hosts, connecting people who have space to spare with those who are looking for a place to stay. Through their experiences on Airbnb, guests and hosts build real connections with real people from all over the globe.

Transportation

For cheap flights to San Diego, be sure to check Southwest Airlines and Virgin America. Don't forget that Amtrak also stops here. For those flying into San Diego, you have several options for getting around the city if you prefer to avoid renting a car. Cabs are quick and inexpensive. We also like car-sharing services like Lyft and Car2Go which have downloadable apps for iphone and android. Finally, consider catching the trolley or bus by accessing MTS for $5/day.
